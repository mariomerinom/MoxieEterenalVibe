## Analysis and Findings

### All dex_arb signals on Arbitrum and Base were phantom

Every signal detected since Phase 4 originated from Solidly-fork pools where our constant-product math (`x * y = k`) does not apply.

| Chain | Signal | Pool Protocol | Our math said | Pool's own `getAmountOut` says | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Arbitrum | wstETH V3 → V2 | Camelot | 2.77 WETH per wstETH | 1.17 WETH per wstETH | Phantom — 137% overestimate |
| Base | wstETH V3 → V2 | Aerodrome | similar pattern | — | Phantom |
| Base | cbETH V3 → V2 | Aerodrome | similar pattern | — | Phantom |
| Base | ezETH V3 → V2 | Aerodrome | similar pattern | — | Phantom |
| Base | rETH V3 → V2 | Aerodrome | similar pattern | — | Phantom |

Camelot and Aerodrome are Solidly forks. For correlated pairs (LST/WETH), they use a stableswap invariant (`x^3*y + x*y^3 = k`) that prices assets much closer together than constant-product. Our math saw a 55% "mispricing" that doesn't exist.

### Simulation framework works but is vacuous without a deployed contract

The `--simulate` cross-check reported 100% pass rate on Arbitrum (256 blocks). Investigation revealed the contract address is `0x000...000` (placeholder default). The EVM calls an empty address, which always succeeds with ~22k gas. A real V3+V2 swap path uses 150k+ gas. The framework itself is sound — it correctly forks state, builds CacheDB, executes transactions. It just needs a real contract target.

### Post-fix signal landscape

After filtering Solidly-fork pools, both chains produce zero signals during steady-state blocks. The remaining cycle universe (1112 Arbitrum, 1452 Base — all V3-V3 and V3-standard-V2 combinations) is active but not currently profitable. Real signals will only appear during price dislocations.
